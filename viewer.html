How to Annotate

Stroke Annotation (Click on Player)

Use this to annotate a specific stroke by a specific player.

  1. Click on a player in any video tile. All videos pause and a red marker appears where you clicked. You can reposition the marker by clicking elsewhere on the video.
  2. Use ← → arrow keys to step frame-by-frame to find the start of the stroke.
  3. Press S to mark the start frame. The video will begin playing at 0.5x speed.
  4. Use Space to pause/resume playback while watching the stroke.
  5. Press E to mark the end frame. The video pauses.
  6. Use ← → to fine-tune the end position frame-by-frame if needed.
  7. Press Enter to confirm and open the annotation form.
  8. Press Esc at any time to cancel and resume normal playback.

Drill Annotation (S/E Time Range)

Use this to annotate a drill segment without marking a specific player position.

  1. While the video is playing, press S or click Drill Start to mark the start time.
  2. Press E or click Drill End to mark the end time.
  3. The annotation form appears automatically.
  4. Select a Drill type (Ground Strokes, Net Play, or Serves). Action can be set to "None" for drill-only annotations.

Annotation Form

  • Player — Select the player from the roster dropdown.
  • Action — Choose the stroke type (forehand, backhand, serve, etc.) or "None" for drill-only annotations.
  • Drill — Optionally select a drill type: Ground Strokes, Net Play, or Serves.
  • Perfect stroke — Check this box if the stroke was executed perfectly.
  • Notes — Add any free-form observations.

Playback Controls

  • Space — Play / Pause all videos
  • Speed buttons — 0.5x, 1x, 2x playback speed
  • Seek slider — Drag to jump to any point in the session
  • Volume — Adjust per-video using the slider below each tile
  • Minimize — Hover over a video and click the − button to shrink it to the tray. Click the minimized video or + to restore it. Remaining videos expand to fill the space.

Saving

Click Save to Dropbox to upload your annotations. They are saved as both JSON and CSV, and will auto-load next time you open this session.
